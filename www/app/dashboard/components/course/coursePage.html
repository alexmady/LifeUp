Resilience:
You can't control what your mind thinks but you can identify unhelpful thoughts and choose whether to take them personally or not Unhelpful emotions are caused by taking unhelpful thoughts personally. You can release an unhelpful emotion by paying attention to the sensation the emotion creates in the body The trick to changing how you feel, is to first accept how you feel and then to de-personalise the unhelpful thoughts driving the feelings.
Connection:
When caught up in thought, we spend most of our time 'somewhere else’. It's like watching a film about life, rather than being there and experiencing it. This can lead to feeling disconnected and disillusioned with life. The secret to feeling a deep connection and satisfaction with life is to meet it where it resides, in the moment (not the past or future). This is achieved by managing your mind's disposition to sit in the middle of you and life; to experience life directly rather than as a second hand story as told by your mind
Authenticity:
Awareness of our own judgements and interpretations (be they helpful or unhelpful), leading to improved relationships, empathy and peace of mind. Gain a clearer picture of what we want from life - be authentic in our behaviour and actions. Live with purpose.

Each stage consists of two steps, these are:

  1. Resilience: DISARM & SPACE
  2. Connection: FLOW & ACT
  3. Authenticity: BE & I

By following the LifeUp 6 steps of mindfulness you can reach and stay on top of the mountain and life.

To help you on your journey, each step is also a full technique encapsulated in an acronym. So for example the first step is DISARM and the first letter in the acronym DISARM is D, which stands for Discover unhelpful thoughts.
