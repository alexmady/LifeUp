Smile - it's proven to make you feel good
SPACE: Smile, Perspective, Appreciation and forgiveness, Connect, Emotions
Create head space by using the SPACE technique to rest & relax and allow your happiness and creativity to emanate through.
S is for Smile - it's free!
Education
What is headspace?
If you can sense an alert inner stillness in the background while things happen in the foreground - that’s your headspace. Remember, you can’t find it as if you were looking for an object, because it isn't an object, it’s space. However you can connect with it!
Amazingly, if you want to connect with your headspace the first step is to just simply smile. Got nothing to smile about? Don't fret - it's scientifically proven that you can 'fake it till you make it'!
Research shows that the simple act of turning your mouth up, whether authentic or not, can help release endorphins, the feel-good hormones and serotonin, a chemical that’s a natural stress-reducer.
Many top executives use the technique of smiling in the mirror each morning, as a way to create space and start the day in the right mindset.
Remember that smiling is not only free but it's contagious! When you smile the world smiles with you…
Activity
  1. Take a few breaths then smile playfully as if you just remembered something funny. Focus on the upturned corners of your mouth.
  2. Notice how it relaxes the entire face and head, and starts to make you feel good inside!
  3. Now do it again and as you do it try to think of something negative without losing the smile. It's hard. When we smile our body is sending the rest of us a message that life is good.
Take away
Don’t take yourself too seriously – have a smile to get some headspace!
Perspective - time to get some
SPACE: Smile, Perspective, Appreciation and forgiveness, Connect, Emotions
P is for Perspective - time to get some!
Education
We can sometimes get so caught up in ourselves that we can lose perspective on a situation and/or our life.
‘Reframing’ our perspective is a great way to access our headspace. Reframing is very much like changing the frame on a picture - the situation hasn’t changed, but how you view it has.
Of course, It’s not about pretending that everything in your life is wonderful and perfect, it's about seeing the situation how it really is, rather than through our mental interpretations.
There are two broad approaches to reframing - content and visual reframing:
  1. Content reframing is achieved by proactively 'coaching' ourselves into perspective e.g. ‘What would I say to a friend who had this problem?’
  2. Visual reframing uses the sheer vastness of the natural world to provide space and to expand perspective.
Activity
Choose one or more of the following perspective techniques and give it a try!
Content Perspective Reframing:
Scan your mind for uncomfortable or repetitive thoughts and emotions. Ask yourself, ‘What is it that is bothering me today?’
  1. How will I feel about this problem in a month, a year, 10 years, 50 years?
  2. What would I say to a friend who had this problem?
  3. What would I do, if I only had a short time to live?
  4. What is the worst that could happen? Go with this thought. What would that look like? How would you recover? Would it really be so bad?
Visual Perspective reframing:
  1. Look out the window and observe the scene, whether it's the vastness of the sky out of your train window, the busy people in the city or the scenery of your garden. Just look without thought for a moment. Or even, better step outside!
  2. Visualise where you are now, then zoom out to see the building, then the street, the city, the country, the continent, the entire earth, then the universe. Stay there in the vast nothingness. Pause.
If you did the exercise, give yourself a ‘mini high five’!
Take away
Remember, life is full of ups and downs. By changing the way we look at things we can gain perspective and uncover our headspace.
Appreciation and Forgiveness - the keys to joy and freedom
SPACE: Smile Perspective, Appreciation and forgiveness, Connect, Emotions
A is for Appreciation and forgiveness – the keys to joy and freedom.
Education
Regularly practising appreciation and forgiveness really is a good way to access your headspace and make you feel great.
When we consciously or unconsciously hold a grudge we are pulled into past or future thoughts of what someone said /didn’t say, or did /didn’t do. These trigger related emotions and conceal our underlying headspace, that quiet, still part of your mind that doesn't judge, unlike the constant thought chatter.
The key to forgiveness is that it actually has nothing to do with the other person – it doesn’t even matter if they don’t know you have forgiven them! We forgive them for our own sake, to be able to access headspace and a place of calm again.
Forgiveness is not about making the other person right or condoning their behaviour. It is about releasing yourself from any distress or discomfort the situation is causing you.
You'll be surprised how many little grudges and grievances we can hold onto on an average day, many of which we may not be consciously aware of but are contributing to our background feeling of negativity and annoyance.
Activity
Try the following
Appreciation:
To bring a sense of appreciation inside of you, think of three things in your life, big or small, that you really appreciate.
Forgiveness:
You don't need to focus on particular grudges you have. To access your headspace just repeat the following affirmation a few times internally with genuine and humble sincerity. Try it now:
“I forgive everyone and I forgive myself for my own sake. I choose to let go of the silliness and resentment so I can live free of its burden.”
Take away
Forgiveness is not about making the other person right or condoning their behaviour. It is about releasing yourself from any distress or discomfort the situation is causing you.
By practising appreciation and forgiveness you can access your headspace and happiness and feel great!
